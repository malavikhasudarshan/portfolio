Bumble x Adobe Designathon - Awarded 2nd Place

Final Pitch Deck

In the 2023 Bumble x Adobe Express Designathon, our team reimagined Bumble’s app experience to address a pressing challenge:

How might Bumble help college students feel comfortable enough to take their digital connections offline?

Our Team and Approach

Our team, comprised of Berkeley undergraduates—myself, Madelyn Christensen, Anishi Patel, and Sanjana Nitturkar—collaborated closely over 7 hours to deliver an innovative, user-centered solution. Using Figma as our brainstorming and prototyping platform, we created two user personas to identify key pain points, crafted a draft Instagram post using Adobe Express to showcase our vision, and developed features focused on enhancing safety and facilitating seamless connections.

Our Insights

We interviewed peers to understand why conversations with matches often fizzle out. The feedback was clear:
  • Asking someone out too soon can feel rushed, making it hard to continue the conversation.
  • Waiting too long to ask leaves both parties unsure how to transition to meeting offline.

    • Proposed Features

  • The Green Light to Meet Button: Allows users to express interest in meeting up by inputting a date idea. When both parties are ready, the button turns green, signaling mutual interest.
  • Well-Beeing Safety Checklist: An optional feature that offers a risk assessment for the planned date and safety tips.
  • The Buzz Word System: Combines audio tracking, trigger phrase recognition, and a discreet alert system. In unsafe situations, users can activate an emergency response without anyone noticing.

    • Outcome

    Our prototype and presentation, designed in Figma and Canva, earned us 2nd place out of 64 students, with judges praising our creative approach and polished UI.
